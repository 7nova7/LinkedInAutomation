An AI recommended a restaurant that closed two years ago to a traveler in a foreign city.

That's the moment I realized the hardest part of building AI products isn't the model. It's everything around it.

I built Roamly — an AI-powered trip planner that takes a destination and travel dates and generates a full day-by-day itinerary. But not just text. Every stop plotted on a 3D interactive map with animated camera flights between locations and color-coded routes for each day.

I presented it at our university's Agentic & GenAI Fair, and the biggest lessons had nothing to do with the AI itself.

The prompt is the actual product. Getting Gemini to return structured JSON with real coordinates, realistic time slots, and accurate descriptions took more iteration than building the entire frontend. One wrong field and the whole map breaks.

AI hallucinations aren't just bugs — they're trust issues. So I wired in Google Places API as a verification layer to confirm every recommended location actually exists.

The messiest part? I burned through three AI coding tools building this — Lovable for rapid prototyping, Claude Code for mid-stage logic, OpenAI Codex for later features. Each had strengths. None was enough alone.

Roamly is live, open source, and has a demo video if you want to see the 3D map in action.

If you're building with AI right now — what's the one thing that turned out to be way harder than you expected?
